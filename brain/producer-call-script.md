# Producer Call Script

## Opener (before anything else)

> "Hi [name], I'm Noah Fisher. We're launching a tart Greek yogurt brand at the University of Florida this fall, and we're looking for a co-packer for our pilot run. We need a ~60–100 gallon batch in 16oz pints, production in August. What's your minimum order?"

**Why we ask this first:** Saves time. If they need 1,000+ gallons, we move on.

---

## If they're interested (and minimum is ≤100 gal):

### Product spec

"Our formula is:
- 300g Greek yogurt (2%, Fage preferred)
- 50g whole-milk kefir
- 40g honey
- 12g fresh lemon juice
- 1g sea salt

Total mix per pint: ~403g. We want a noticeably tart profile (~4.2–4.4 pH), dense low-overrun texture (30–50%), and clean ingredients — no stabilizers or gums. Does your facility handle cultured dairy and custom batches?"

### Licensing & capacity

"Are you FDA-licensed and insured for wholesale packaged frozen dessert in Florida? Do you have freezer capacity for ~350 16oz pints in August?"

### Timeline & economics

"We need production Aug 1–7, delivery to us or Snag dark store (Gainesville, FL). What's your per-pint all-in cost including ingredients we supply? Our wholesale price to retail is $3.25/pint, so we need headroom."

### Custom packaging

"We have 16oz cups coming custom-printed with our label. You'd fill from our cups. That OK?"

### Next steps if yes

"Great. Can you send us a formal quote by [DATE] with: your all-in per-pint cost, production timeline, deposit terms, and delivery options? We're calling 3 facilities in parallel, so speed matters."
